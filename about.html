Where I'm From

I was born and raised in Providence, Rhode Island.

For most of high school, I attended a private school on the east side. However, my junior year I studied and lived abroad in Zaragoza, Spain.

I grew up in a bi-cultural home. My mother is American and an immigration lawyer. My father is Salvadoran and my step-father is Cuban.

Where I Went To School

In 2007 I enrolled at George Washington University in Washington, D.C.

There I studied Spanish Literature and minor in Applied Ethics (a branch of Philosophy) and Sociology.

While there, I also worked for the university baseball team, traveling on the road with them and attending all games and practices.

I graduated in the winter of 2010 (a semester early).

After College

After college I took interships with the Tampa Bay Rays and the Miami Marlins where I worked in the player development/baseball operations departments.
